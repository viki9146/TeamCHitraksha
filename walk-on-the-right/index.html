Walk On The Right

The "Walk on the Right" movement is a joint initiative by the Nanded Police and the Regional Transport Office (RTO) of Nanded, aimed at promoting pedestrian safety and reducing road accidents. This movement is focused on educating the public about the importance of walking on the right side of the road, especially in crowded areas and near pedestrian crossings.

This movement was initiated in response to the growing number of road accidents involving pedestrians, particularly in areas with heavy foot traffic. By promoting the concept of walking on the right, the Nanded Police and RTO aim to reduce the chances of collisions between pedestrians and vehicles. Additionally, this movement also emphasizes the importance of pedestrian safety and encourages people to be more aware of their surroundings while walking on the road.

The "Walk on the Right" movement involves several awareness drives and campaigns aimed at educating the public about the importance of walking on the right side of the road. This includes workshops and seminars for school children, distribution of flyers and posters, and road safety demonstrations. The Nanded Police and RTO also work closely with local organizations and community groups to spread the message of this movement to a wider audience.

In addition to promoting pedestrian safety, the "Walk on the Right" movement also aims to raise awareness about traffic laws and regulations. The Nanded Police and RTO work closely with local authorities to ensure that the road infrastructure is equipped with proper pedestrian crossings, zebra crossings, and footpaths to make it safer for people to walk on the road.
